# Chapter 1
A much better way to practice is to start directly at the spots that are challenging for you. Even if you think, “Well, I don’t know where those are,” think harder. You do know.  

Once you’ve identified the specific problem, figure out how to break down the problem into smaller, more manageable parts.  

***By the way, I heard Nick Finzer says this: don't practice something until you get it right, practice something until you can't get it wrong."***  

We need to ensure that the correct pathway gets reinforced many more times than the incorrect pathway.  

10 times correctly in a row.

**My comment would be like it is the exactly opposite of playing Souls-Like game. you beat the boss once doesn't mean that you can actually master the boss... but if you can beat the boss 10 times correctly in a row... that might mean you actually dominant the game.**    

The more times a signal passes through, the more wraps of myelin are put on, making the skill feel easier and easier. Things that feel effortless and automatic have many wraps of myelin, which is another reason repetitions are important: they increase myelin wraps.
